⚡The problem GridGuard solves
▼
Cities around the world are adding solar panels and electric vehicles at record speed. This creates a hidden challenge: supply and demand on the grid must stay roughly balanced at all times. Too much demand causes blackouts. Too much supply causes overgeneration, a real issue that forced California to shut down solar farms at midday because the grid could not absorb the excess. Professional tools to test this cost thousands of dollars. GridGuard does it for free, in a browser, in under a minute.
🔢The math behind the simulation
▼
Supply = (panels × 0.3 × solar multiplier) + (turbines × 2 × wind multiplier)
EV load = EVs × 1.5 × EV multiplier
Total load = 60 kW base household use + EV load
Balance = supply minus load
Risk = if balance is negative, how severe is the deficit as a percentage of grid capacity

Event buttons change the multipliers. EV rush hour sets EV multiplier to 3. Solar noon sets solar multiplier to 2. Wind drop sets wind multiplier to 0.2.
🎨What the four colors mean
▼
🟢 Green: Stable. Supply exceeds load. No action needed.

🟡 Amber: Stressed. Load slightly exceeds supply. The grid is coping but fragile. One more EV could tip it over.

🔴 Red: Overload. Load significantly exceeds supply. In a real grid this causes voltage drops and blackouts.

🔵 Blue: Overgeneration. Supply is far more than load. Real grids must dump or export excess power or risk equipment damage. Too much is also a problem.

🏠 Energy at Home
🌡️Adjust your thermostat by 2°C
High impact▼
Set your AC to 22°C instead of 20°C. In the GCC region, every degree saved reduces your cooling load by roughly 6%. This is the single biggest lever most households have and requires no equipment purchase.
🕐Run appliances at off-peak times
High impact▼
Washing machines, dishwashers, and dryers create significant load. Running them after 9pm or before 7am avoids morning and evening demand peaks when the grid is most stressed. This is exactly the kind of behavior shift GridGuard is designed to make visible.
💡Replace all bulbs with LED
Medium impact▼
LED bulbs use 75% less energy than traditional bulbs and last 25 times longer. For a 4-person household switching 20 bulbs, you can save 30 to 50 kWh per month with a one-time purchase cost under SAR 200.
🔌Kill standby power
Low impact▼
Devices on standby, including TVs, gaming consoles, and phone chargers left plugged in, silently consume 5 to 10% of your electricity. Smart power strips automatically cut standby drain without any daily effort needed.
🚗 Electric Vehicles
🌙Charge your EV overnight, not after work
High impact▼
If millions of EV owners plug in at 6pm when they get home, they create a massive simultaneous demand spike, exactly when the the grid is already under pressure from cooking and lighting. Overnight charging between 11pm and 6am avoids this completely and is usually cheaper due to off-peak rates.
☀️Charge from solar when available
High impact▼
If you have solar panels, schedule EV charging during peak solar hours (10am to 2pm) when your panels generate the most power. This uses energy that would otherwise be exported to the grid or go unused, making your EV effectively free to run on sunny days.
🤝 Community Action
🏘️Propose a neighborhood solar scheme
High impact▼
Community solar projects allow buildings that cannot install their own panels, such as apartments or rental properties, to share the output of a shared installation. One shared rooftop can offset energy costs for an entire floor of a building and qualifies for collective subsidies unavailable to individual homeowners.
📱Monitor your consumption with a smart app
Medium impact▼
Apps connected to smart meters let you see your usage hour by hour. Awareness alone typically reduces consumption by 5 to 15% simply by surfacing waste you did not know existed. The same principle GridGuard uses to show grid state, applied to your own home.

From hackathon demo to national infrastructure

Every government pursuing net-zero emissions faces the same problem: the public does not understand the grid, so they cannot participate in decisions about it. GridGuard bridges that gap by making real energy tradeoffs visible, interactive, and free for everyone.
